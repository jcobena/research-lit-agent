# 🔬 Biomarkers in HIV: A Literature-Based Overview

Biomarkers in HIV span **diagnosis, disease progression monitoring, treatment response, comorbidity risk, and prognosis**. Below is a synthesis of current knowledge grounded in recent literature.

---

## 1. 🧪 Classic Immunological Biomarkers

### ▸ CD4+ T Cell Count
CD4+ T cell count remains the primary biomarker for immune status and disease staging. It guides when to initiate ART and assess immune recovery.

### ▸ CD4/CD8 Ratio
Even after viral suppression on ART, a persistently **low CD4/CD8 ratio** signals ongoing immune dysregulation and risk for non-AIDS comorbidities. Studies on **fecal microbiota transplantation (FMT)** are exploring ways to improve this ratio by modulating gut dysbiosis and reducing T-cell activation (PMID: [41390455](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41390455/)).

### ▸ CD8+ T Cell Expansion
Persistent **CD8+ T cell elevation** despite successful ART indicates residual immune activation and has been associated with increased comorbidity risk (PMID: [41737216](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41737216/)).

---

## 2. 🦠 Viral Biomarkers

### ▸ HIV Viral Load (Plasma RNA)
The cornerstone of treatment monitoring. Viral suppression (<50 copies/mL) is the primary therapeutic goal.

### ▸ HIV-DNA (Reservoir Marker)
**HIV-DNA** in peripheral blood cells reflects the **latent viral reservoir**. Studies of long-acting ART (cabotegravir/rilpivirine) show changes in HIV-DNA dynamics and immune profiles over time (PMID: [40312985](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/40312985/)). However, the relationship between HIV reservoir size and chronic inflammation appears complex — one study found **no significant association** between reservoir size and inflammatory markers in long-term virally suppressed individuals (PMID: [40791593](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/40791593/)).

---

## 3. 🔥 Inflammatory & Immune Activation Biomarkers

Chronic inflammation persists even with effective ART and is a major driver of non-AIDS morbidities.

| Biomarker | Role |
|---|---|
| **CRP (C-reactive protein)** | Systemic inflammation marker; predicts mortality in HIV/AIDS-related opportunistic infections (PMID: [41452335](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41452335/)) |
| **IL-6, IL-10, TNF-α** | Pro-inflammatory cytokines elevated in HIV disease progression, lymphoma risk, and helminth co-infection (PMID: [41130223](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41130223/); [34131000](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/34131000/)) |
| **sCD14 (soluble CD14)** | Marker of **monocyte activation** and microbial translocation from the gut; linked to cardiovascular and inflammatory comorbidities (PMID: [41130223](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41130223/)) |
| **IL-10, CXCL13** | Prognostic biomarkers for AIDS-related non-Hodgkin Lymphoma (ARL); predict treatment response and survival (PMID: [34131000](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/34131000/)) |

---

## 4. 🧬 Emerging & Novel Biomarkers

### ▸ Circulating microRNAs (miRNAs)
miRNAs are small non-coding RNAs whose expression profiles are altered during HIV infection. They show promise for **dual-purpose use in HIV diagnosis and prognosis**, including detecting ART resistance (PMID: [40457240](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/40457240/)). They also feature prominently in **HIV-associated lymphoma (HAL)** risk stratification (PMID: [40723865](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/40723865/)).

### ▸ Cell-Free Mitochondrial DNA (ccf-mtDNA)
Released into the bloodstream following cell death, **ccf-mtDNA** is an emerging marker of cellular damage and immune dysregulation. Its dynamics in both **acute and chronic HIV infection** have been recently characterized in human and primate models (PMID: [41556528](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41556528/)).

### ▸ Extracellular Vesicles (EVs)
EVs carry disease-specific molecular cargo and are emerging as novel biomarkers for **early risk prediction and prognostic evaluation** in HIV-associated malignancies (PMID: [40723865](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/40723865/)).

### ▸ Angiopoietin-2
In HIV patients with disseminated histoplasmosis, **Angiopoietin-2** demonstrated **high specificity** for mortality prediction (PMID: [41452335](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41452335/)).

---

## 5. 💊 Biomarkers in the Context of ART

Longitudinal studies show that **INSTI-based ART regimens** (e.g., dolutegravir, bictegravir) differentially affect inflammatory and metabolic biomarker panels over 48 weeks, which may have implications for long-term comorbidity risk (PMID: [41706710](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41706710/)).

**Dyslipidemia markers** (LDL, HDL, triglycerides) are also critical biomarkers in PWH given the increased cardiovascular risk associated with both HIV and ART (PMID: [40436702](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/40436702/)).

---

## 6. 🧒 Biomarkers in Special Populations

### ▸ HIV-Exposed Uninfected (HEU) Children
Perinatal HIV exposure, even without infection, leads to **long-term alterations in immune marker levels**, underscoring the importance of monitoring this growing population (PMID: [41747457](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41747457/)).

### ▸ HIV and Depression
**Pro-inflammatory biomarkers** (elevated IL-6, CRP, TNF-α) are significantly associated with **depression severity** in PWH, highlighting a neuro-immune intersection that may affect ART adherence and outcomes (PMID: [41014912](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41014912/)).

## 🔑 Key Takeaway
Biomarkers in HIV have evolved far beyond CD4 count and viral load. The field is rapidly advancing toward **multidimensional biomarker panels** incorporating inflammatory markers, viral reservoir measures, circulating nucleic acids, and metabolomics to enable **precision medicine** approaches in the management of HIV and its comorbidities.
