# 🔬 Biomarkers in HIV Infection

Biomarkers in HIV are measurable biological indicators used to **diagnose infection, monitor disease progression, assess immune status, guide treatment, and predict outcomes**. They span several categories:

---

## 1. 🧫 Immunological Biomarkers

### **CD4+ T-Cell Count**
- The **CD4+ T-cell count** is the cornerstone biomarker for assessing immune function in HIV-infected individuals.
- HIV causes progressive **CD4+ T-cell depletion**, leading to immunodeficiency.
- CD4 nadir (lowest recorded CD4 count) is strongly associated with long-term immune recovery and clinical outcomes (PMID: **39894201**).

### **CD4/CD8 Ratio**
- A declining **CD4/CD8 ratio** is a key marker of immune dysfunction and disease progression.
- Even in virologically suppressed patients on antiretroviral therapy (ART), a persistently low CD4/CD8 ratio indicates ongoing immune activation and risk of comorbidities (PMID: **40111214**, **41390455**).

### **Lymphocyte Activation Markers**
- Activated **CD8+ and CD19+ lymphocytes** are associated with HIV reservoir size, immune dysfunction, and non-AIDS comorbidities (PMID: **39894201**).

---

## 2. 🧬 Virological Biomarkers

### **Plasma Viral Load (HIV RNA)**
- **HIV-1 viral load** (copies/mL of plasma) is a primary marker of viral replication and treatment efficacy.
- Undetectable viral load indicates successful ART, while elevated viral load signals treatment failure or drug resistance.

### **HIV DNA Reservoir**
- **HIV-DNA levels** (measured by droplet digital PCR) quantify the viral reservoir in latently infected cells.
- The size of the HIV-1 reservoir is linked to immune activation markers and clinical outcomes in virologically suppressed patients (PMID: **39894201**).

### **Residual Plasma Viremia**
- Even during ART, low-level residual virus persists and is associated with ongoing immune activation (PMID: **39894201**).

---

## 3. 🔥 Inflammatory & Immune Activation Biomarkers

### **Cytokines (e.g., IL-6)**
- **Interleukin-6 (IL-6)** and other **senescence-associated secretory phenotype (SASP) factors** are elevated in people with HIV (PWH) and reflect chronic inflammation and cellular aging (PMID: **41797680**).

### **Cellular Senescence Markers**
- **SA-β-Galactosidase (SA-βGal), p16INK4a, γH2AX, and Bcl-2** are classical cellular senescence biomarkers that are elevated in PWH, even on ART, reflecting accelerated aging (PMID: **41797680**).

### **Microbial Translocation Markers**
- Gut dysbiosis and **microbial translocation** contribute to systemic T-cell activation in HIV, detectable through markers such as lipopolysaccharide (LPS) and soluble CD14 (PMID: **41390455**).

---

## 4. 🧪 Novel & Emerging Biomarkers

### **Circulating Cell-Free DNA (ccfDNA)**
- **ccfDNA** has emerged as a promising biomarker for immune function and disease progression in HIV-1-infected individuals, offering a non-invasive measure of cellular damage (PMID: **41630058**).

### **Extracellular Vesicles (EVs)**
- The **size and abundance of EVs** in plasma are inversely correlated with the CD4/CD8 ratio in people with HIV, making them useful biomarkers for monitoring disease progression.
- EVs enriched with **microRNA-155 (miR-155)** have been shown to enhance HIV-1 infection and are detectable in blood (PMID: **40111214**).

### **Human Endogenous Retroviruses (HERVs)**
- **HERVs** (ancient retroviral sequences in the human genome) can be reactivated by HIV-1 and are associated with HIV reservoir size, CD4 count, and lymphocyte activation — positioning them as potential novel clinical monitoring biomarkers (PMID: **39894201**).

### **Circulating miRNAs**
- **MicroRNAs** (e.g., miR-155) found in EVs and plasma are being studied as markers of disease activity, reservoir size, and immune dysregulation (PMID: **40723865**).

---

## 5. 🎗️ Biomarkers in HIV-Associated Malignancy

In **HIV-associated lymphoma (HAL)**, a multidimensional panel of biomarkers is used including:
- **Inflammatory cytokine profiles**
- **Serum protein profiles**
- **Lymphocyte subsets**
- **Extracellular vesicles & circulating miRNAs**
- **Viral biomarkers**

These support early risk prediction, therapeutic monitoring, and prognostic stratification (PMID: **40723865**).

## 🔑 Key Takeaway
Biomarkers in HIV range from **classic clinical markers** (CD4 count, viral load) to **novel molecular biomarkers** (EVs, ccfDNA, HERVs, miRNAs). Despite effective ART, persistent immune activation and inflammation require ongoing monitoring using an expanding repertoire of biomarkers that help stratify risk, guide therapy, and detect comorbidities early.
